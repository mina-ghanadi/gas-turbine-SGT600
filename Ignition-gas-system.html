Ignition gas system

Purpose of the system

The purpose of the system is to supply the ignition gas burner with the right amount of gas and air so the main flame can be ignited. The purpose of the system is also to supply burner no. 6 with gas during ignition of the gas turbine.

General description of the system

Gas for the ignition gas unit is supplied from main gas system or from gas bottles (Propane). The gas then flows through a pressure reducing valve, two shut-off valves, one flow control valve and an orifice into the ignition burner. Instrument air enters the system from an external supply system and flows through one manual shut off valve, a pressure reducing valve, a flow control valve and two-shut off valves to supply the ignition burner with air. Burner No. 6 is supplied with gas that flows through one manual control valve/orifice, two automatic shut-off valves and into burner no. 6.

Technical Specification

Dimensioning data Fuel gas flow required 0,0012 kg/s (5,2 Nm3/h) for ignition gas burner and 0,005 kg/s (21,7 Nm3/h) for burner no. 6. Air flow 30 Nm3/h. The 24 VDC system is powered from the 230 VAC system.
